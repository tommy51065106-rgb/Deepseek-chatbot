Creatine and muscle strength evidence summary
Source context: Wang et al. 2024, Nutrients. Population: healthy adults younger than 50. Intervention: creatine monohydrate combined with structured resistance training. Study quality in the source summary: PEDro 7.0.

Main efficacy finding
Creatine plus resistance training significantly improved strength compared with placebo plus resistance training in adults under 50. Reported average gains were about 4.43 kg for upper-body strength and 11.35 kg for lower-body strength.

Sex-specific finding in this analysis
In this specific meta-analysis, statistically significant effects were clearer in males. Reported values were around 4.95 kg upper-body and 11.68 kg lower-body. Female subgroup estimates were positive but did not reach statistical significance in that dataset.

Practical dosage ranges used in the literature
Typical loading phase: 15 to 25 grams per day for 5 to 7 days.
Typical maintenance phase: 2 to 10 grams per day.
Some analyses suggest maintenance above 5 grams per day may produce larger lower-body effects than maintenance at or below 5 grams per day.

Likely mechanisms
Creatine may improve repeated high-intensity performance through faster phosphocreatine recovery and ATP resynthesis. It may also improve metabolic buffering during intense effort and support training quality over time.

Implementation note
Benefits are most reliable when creatine is paired with progressive, structured resistance training rather than taken alone.
