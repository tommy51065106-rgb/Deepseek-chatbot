Fitness fundamentals

Progressive overload is the core principle for strength and muscle gain. Increase weight, repetitions, total sets, or training density gradually over time while maintaining good technique.

Cardio training should match the goal. Use zone 2 cardio for aerobic base building, interval work for conditioning, and sport-specific efforts for performance carryover.

Recovery is part of training. Sleep 7 to 9 hours, keep protein intake consistent, and avoid increasing training volume too aggressively when fatigue is already high.

Fat loss depends primarily on a sustainable calorie deficit. Preserve muscle with resistance training and adequate protein, and use cardio as support rather than the only strategy.

Beginners usually benefit most from full-body training 3 times per week with squat, hinge, push, pull, and carry patterns practiced consistently.
