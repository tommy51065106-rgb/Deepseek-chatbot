Source context: Vasquez-Bonilla et al. 2023, Research Quarterly for Exercise and Sport.Main efficacy findingIncreased muscle oxygenation asymmetry ($MO_2Asy$) and elevated training workloads are significantly correlated with a higher probability of injury. Monitoring metabolic health at rest provides a window into injury risk:Asymmetry Risk: An $MO_2Asy$ greater than 15% at the end of an occlusion serves as a primary predictor for injury.Workload Synergy: The predictive value of oxygenation metrics is most effective when paired with internal workload data (e.g., Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio).Injury Prevalence: Non-contact injuries affected 30.7% of the cohort, with the knee being the most frequent site of injury in males (41.6%).Specific findings in this analysisThe study identifies sex-specific metabolic markers and anatomical risks:Male Athletes: Significant injury risk is identified when $MO_2Asy$ exceeds 15% and the oxidative capacity ($\Delta SmO_2$ Slope 2) exceeds 9%. Males generally exhibit higher absolute oxygen consumption ratios than females.Female Athletes: Injured players exhibit higher $MO_2Asy$ compared to uninjured peers, particularly in high-severity cases.Metabolic Asymmetry: Differences in oxygen saturation between the dominant and non-dominant legs serve as a critical red flag for musculoskeletal vulnerability.Practical dosage and protocol ranges used in the literature$MO_2Asy$ Threshold: Maintain asymmetry below 15% to minimize injury probability.Oxidative Capacity ($\Delta SmO_2$ Slope 2): Monitor for shifts exceeding 9% in males as a marker of altered phosphocreatine (PCr) resynthesis.Monitoring Period: Data suggests high utility during 4-week pre-season blocks to identify "excessive use" risks before the competitive season begins.Assessment Tool: Use of Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (NIRS) at rest to evaluate $SmO_2$ levels.Likely mechanismsVascular Regulation: Asymmetries may be driven by impaired nitric oxide (nNOS) expression, hindering the ability of microvessels to dilate during hyperemic responses.Metabolic Deficits: Injuries are often preceded by underlying reductions in mitochondrial respiratory capacity and muscle metabolism.Capillary Density: Lower $SmO_2$ values typically indicate a reduced number of capillaries, limiting the cardiovascular system's ability to deliver oxygen to active tissues.Implementation notePractitioners should integrate NIRS-derived oxygenation monitoring into a holistic fatigue management system. Specifically, $MO_2Asy$ should be cross-referenced with Acute:Chronic Workload Ratios (ACWR) to differentiate between expected training adaptations and dangerous "overreaching" patterns.
